# MASTER GUIDELINES - Subjective Questions Evaluation

## evaluation_rubric - Standard Scoring Criteria

### excellent_answer - 90-100%
- Mentions ALL core concepts with clear understanding
- Includes 2+ peripheral concepts  
- Makes connections between concepts
- Provides examples or analogies
- Demonstrates depth and breadth

### good_answer - 70-89%
- Mentions MOST core concepts (at least 2/3)
- Includes 1 peripheral concept
- Shows solid understanding
- Minor gaps acceptable

### needs_improvement - 50-69%
- Mentions SOME core concepts (at least 1/3)
- May have minor inaccuracies
- Basic understanding evident
- Requires follow-up probing

### insufficient_answer - 0-49%
- Misses most core concepts
- Shows fundamental misunderstanding
- Too vague or incorrect
- Requires foundation building

## follow_up_patterns - Standard Follow-up Structure

### follow_up_excellent - Depth Probe
- Purpose: Challenge strong candidates further
- Approach: Add complexity, explore edge cases
- Goal: Assess next-level readiness

### follow_up_partial - Guided Probe  
- Purpose: Steer toward missed concepts
- Approach: Provide hints without giving answer
- Goal: Check if candidate can self-correct

### follow_up_weak - Foundation Check
- Purpose: Build from basics
- Approach: Simplify and scaffold
- Goal: Establish fundamental understanding

## timing_guidelines - Interview Time Management

### initial_response
- L3: 2-3 minutes
- L4: 3-4 minutes  
- L5: 4-5 minutes
- L6-L7: 5-7 minutes

### discussion_time
- Baseline question: 5-7 minutes total
- Bar raiser question: 7-10 minutes total

## scoring_weights - Component Weights

### core_concepts
- Weight: 60% of total score
- Must be present for passing grade

### peripheral_concepts  
- Weight: 40% of total score
- Differentiates good from excellent

## interviewer_notes - Common Guidelines

### watch_for
- Confusion between similar concepts
- Over-engineering simple problems
- Missing practical considerations
- Copy-paste knowledge vs understanding

### positive_signals
- Clear explanations with examples
- Acknowledging trade-offs
- Asking clarifying questions
- Self-correction when probed

### red_flags  
- Defensive about mistakes
- Unable to explain reasoning
- Memorized answers without understanding
- Cannot adapt to follow-ups
